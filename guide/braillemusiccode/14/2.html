Music Braille Code (2015)

14. Tremolo

14.2. Repeated-Note Tremolo

The braille sign for repeated-note tremolo, consisting of the prefix ^ and a value indicating repetition in a particular value, is placed after the affected note or the last interval if it is a chord, and must only be separated from it by dots or fingerings. When four or more successive print notes are thus fractioned in the same value, the braille sign may be doubled by writing the second cell of the sign twice.
