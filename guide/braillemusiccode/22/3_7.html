22. Nuances and Verbal Expressions

22.3. Words and Abbreviated Words of Expression

22.3.7. Expressions at the Ends of Braille Lines

It is preferable to place expressions in the lines with the notes to which they are related. If considerable space will be conserved, however, an expression that precedes a note may be positioned at the end of a braille line, followed by a music hyphen, thus separating it from the affected music. (See Example 22.3.3-3.)
