13.3. Longer Slurred Phrases

13.3.3. Layered Slurs

When two levels of phrasing or articulation have been juxtaposed in print, bracket slurs are employed to indicate the larger (phrasing) dimension; single slurs, with the doubled-slur device where appropriate, are employed for the smaller (articulation or bowing) units.
