13. The Slur

13.6. Slurs between Parts

13.6.2. Slurs between Staves

The transfer slur sign "C indicates the point at which a slur that is started in one staff is transferred to another staff. The sign replaces a single slur where a single slur would otherwise be employed. In the midst of a longer slur, it is an additional sign either between a doubled slur and the closing single slur, or between the pair of bracket slurs. Example 13.6.2-1 first illustrates the transfer slur replacing a single slur, then as an additional sign between bracket slurs. When there is doubt about which of two or more in-accord parts may be the intended continuation of the phrase following a transfer slur, the modified sign "C' is placed at the beginning of the continuation. Example 13.6.2-2 illustrates use of this sign.
