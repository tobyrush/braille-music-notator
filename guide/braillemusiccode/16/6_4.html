Music Braille Code (2015)

16. Ornaments

16.6. The Glissando Sign

16.6.4. Glissando through an Empty Measure

When a glissando extends through an otherwise empty measure in a single-line part, the sign must be preceded and followed by a bar line (dots 123) with intervening spaces before and after, to mark off the empty measure. When a glissando extends through an otherwise empty measure in a line in bar-over-bar format or an open score, the glissando sign must be vertically aligned as if it were a note.
