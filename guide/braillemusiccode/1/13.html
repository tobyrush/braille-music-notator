Music Braille Code (2015)

1. General Procedures and Signs

1.13. Print Brackets

Various kinds of brackets that appear in print music are represented in braille by pairs of signs that indicate the beginning and ending points of the brackets. These signs are placed in the music lines without intervening spaces or special octave marks for the notes that follow them. Each sign must be placed on the same line as the sign that it precedes at the beginning or that it follows at the ending.
