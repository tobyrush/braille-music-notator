Music Braille Code (2015)

1. General Procedures and Signs

1.8. Metronome Indications

The metronome indication in braille is an imitation of print usage. The note value (of indefinite pitch in the print) is written as C in the required value; the sign 7 represents the "equals" sign and the metronome setting is written with a single numeric indicator. The order is occasionally varied in print, the number being given before the note, and the braille should follow print. Parentheses that enclose the metronome marking in print are omitted in braille. Where the word "circa" or its equivalent in any language occurs, it should be placed before the metronome mark, whatever may be done in the print.
