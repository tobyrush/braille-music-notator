Music Braille Code (2015)

6. Accidentals and Key Signatures

6.5. Key Signatures

When a key signature consists of one, two, or three accidentals, it is represented in braille by the corresponding number of flat or sharp signs in succession. When it consists of four or more accidentals, the number including the numeric indicator precedes a single flat or sharp sign. A key signature is preceded and followed by a blank space (unless it is followed immediately by a meter signature) and is brailled where it appears in print. The signature at the beginning of each staff in print is omitted after its first appearance in a score. A change of key is placed wherever it occurs and generally should be placed on the same line as the following note. The first note after a key signature requires an octave mark. (See Par. 1.7 regarding key signatures in music headings.)
