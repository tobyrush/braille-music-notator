Music Braille Code (2015)

18. Braille Full- and Part-Measure Repeats, Parallel Movement, and Sequence Abbreviation

18.6. The Parallel Movement Sign

When, in a bar-over-bar score, particularly in keyboard music, one line moves parallel with another at the distance of one or more octaves, the writing of the second part may be abbreviated by substituting for its notes a single octave interval sign, with an appropriate octave mark where the two parts are more than one octave apart. The parallel movement device should be used very sparingly. It is generally used for full measures only. (See Par. 33.6 regarding use of the device in ensemble and orchestral scores.)
