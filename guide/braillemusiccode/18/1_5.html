Music Braille Code (2015)

18. Braille Full- and Part-Measure Repeats, Parallel Movement, and Sequence Abbreviation

18.1. General Use of the Braille Repeat Sign

18.1.5. Doublings with the Repeat Sign

The repeat sign may be used when any doublings are entirely contained within the original passage. It may be used when any doublings are in effect at the start of the passage to be repeated and are continued through and beyond the repetition. It may be used in the rare instance where the doubling is initiated on the first note of the original and terminated on the last note of the repetition. In that case, the termination is shown on the last note of the original passage. The sign may not be used when any doubling is not the same in both the original and the repetition.
