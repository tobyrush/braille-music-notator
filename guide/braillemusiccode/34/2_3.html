34.2. Typical Braille Transcription

34.2.3. Interval Signs and In-Accords

In a part for a set of like instruments of different sizes that is to be performed by one person, concurrent notes of the same value that are printed on different lines or spaces may be shown in braille as intervals. In-accords may be used when two or more different rhythms occur together. Intervals and in-accords are read upward. Concurrent notes for different instruments, however, must be given in separate braille lines.
