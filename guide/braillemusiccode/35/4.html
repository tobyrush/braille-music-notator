Music Braille Code (2015)

35. Solo Songs and Choral Parts

35.4. Repeat Sign for Words or Phrases

The repeat sign for a word or phrase 9 is placed, without intervening spaces, before and after the word or phrase to be repeated. All of the text and all of the associated music must be contained in the same parallel. A run-over of either the word line or the music line may be advisable when use of the word- repetition device results in a significant difference between the lengths of the two lines. Capitalization follows that of the original iteration. Opening punctuation of the original iteration is placed after the opening repeat sign, and closing punctuation of the final iteration before the terminating repeat sign. Other differences in capitalization and punctuation are ignored.

  1. When the text is repeated once, one repeat sign is used before and one after it.
  2. When the text is repeated twice, two successive repeat signs are placed before the text and one after it.
  3. When the text is repeated more than twice, one repeat sign before the text is preceded by a number, with the numeric indicator, showing the number of repetitions. A single repeat sign follows the text.
