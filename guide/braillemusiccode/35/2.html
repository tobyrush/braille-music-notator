Music Braille Code (2015)

35. Solo Songs and Choral Parts

35.2. Syllabic Slurs

In vocal music, single and doubled slurs are used to connect two or more notes that are sung on one syllable of text, doubled slurs normally being employed when the slur involves more than four notes. (See also Par. 35.3.2.) The signs must often be provided in braille when there are no slurs in the print to indicate the connection. These braille signs are not used to indicate phrasing; bracket slurs are used for that purpose, even if there are fewer than four notes included under the print phrasing mark. When a syllabic slur coincides with a tie or a portamento, the slur is placed first.
