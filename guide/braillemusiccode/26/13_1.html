Music Braille Code (2015)

26. Fretted Plucked String Instruments

26.13. Interpretation of Chord Diagrams

26.13.1. Braille Display of Chord Diagrams

The braille equivalent of a chord diagram is achieved by combining the string and finger signs in Table 24 with the fret and barré signs. Unplayed strings are not included in the transcription. Example 26.13.1-1 shows a C major chord for guitar written (a) in ordinary staff and braille notation, and (b) in a chord diagram. Examples 26.13.1-2 and 26.13.1-3 show particular voicings of a D minor chord and an E minor chord, respectively.
