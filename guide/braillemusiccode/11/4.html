Music Braille Code (2015)

11. In-Accord and Measure-Division Signs

11.4. Dividing a Measure between Lines

Whenever an in-accord or measure-division sign occurs at the end of a braille line, that sign signifies that the measure is incomplete, and therefore a music hyphen would be redundant. In order to maintain the cohesion of the parts as much as possible, it is preferable to divide the measure at one of these signs rather than to divide one of the parts by means of a music hyphen, provided that doing so does not result in unnecessary additional braille lines.
