11. In-Accord and Measure-Division Signs

11.1. In-Accord Procedures

11.1.2. Part-Measure In-Accords

When the music of a measure is too long or too complicated to be clearly shown as a full-measure in-accord, it is advisable to divide the measure into convenient sections, each section being treated as an isolated unit. The measure-division sign stands between the sections without intervening spaces. The part-measure in-accord sign joins the parts of the resulting section. The music on each side of the in-accord sign must contain exactly the same total note value. In many cases, the transcriber must arbitrarily choose between a full-measure in-accord and a divided measure employing one or more part-measure in-accords. Only a part-measure in-accord may be used for an incomplete measure.
