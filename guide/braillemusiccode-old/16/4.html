Music Braille Code (2015)

16. Ornaments

16.4. Turns and Inverted Turns

The sign for a turn or an inverted turn is placed before the affected note or interval, regardless of its position in print. If a turn is shown affecting a note represented by an interval sign, the braille turn sign is placed before the interval sign. The position of the symbol relative to the note is, however, indicated by the braille sign itself. The basic turn sign 4 indicates a turn that is printed following the note to be ornamented. An inverted turn is represented by the basic sign followed by dots 123. Either turn symbol printed directly above or below the note is represented by the relevant sign preceded by dot 6.
