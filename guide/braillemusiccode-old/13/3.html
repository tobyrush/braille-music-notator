Music Braille Code (2015)

13. The Slur

13.3. Longer Slurred Phrases

When a slur extends over more than four notes, it can be presented in braille in either of two ways. It can be shown by doubling the single slur after the first note of the phrase (and its associated fingerings or intervals) and marking the conclusion of the slur by inserting a single slur after the next-to-last note. It can also be shown by enclosing the slurred notes between a pair of signs ;B and ^2 that together are called bracket slurs.
