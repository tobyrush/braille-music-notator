Music Braille Code (2015)

29. Keyboard Instruments

29.5. Coincidence of Notes

In a florid passage or an unmeasured passage, it may be necessary or useful to show that particular notes coincide between the hands. The coincidence may be shown in one of two ways: (a) the coincidence sign ;2 may be placed before the notes in each part, or (b) the notes may be vertically aligned in each hand part, leaving spaces in the music lines, filling with guide dots (3) if a space exceeds six cells. When a coincidence occurs at the point of division in a measure that is divided between parallels, the coincidence sign is unnecessary.
