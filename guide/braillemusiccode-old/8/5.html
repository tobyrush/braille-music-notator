Music Braille Code (2015)

8. Grouping of Notes

8.5. Three- or Four-Cell Sign for Irregular Groups

The three-cell sign (or four-cell if the number is greater than nine), consisting of dots 456 followed by a lower-cell numeral (without a numeric indicator) and a dot 3, is used to indicate an irregular group consisting of any number of notes other than three. The sign may be doubled for four or more successive like groups, by brailling the dots 456 and numeral twice followed by one dot 3. The braille note-grouping procedure may be employed when the notes are all of the same value.
